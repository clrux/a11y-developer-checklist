WCAG AA Checklist

Historic checks

The Checklist

  • I have written textual alternatives for all non-visual, non-decorative content.

  • I have provided alternative text for all video and audio (if applicable).

  • My video has closed-captioning (if video is present).

  • My video has audio descriptions (if video is present).

  • My markup is logical and has semantic structure, and I've used headings and other landmarks properly.

  • My content is structured in a meaningful order and floated elements (if present) don't interrupt flow from top to bottom.

  • I provide content and instructions in such a way that no one sense alone is required.

  • My information is presented in such a way that color alone is not the only way to understand it.

  • I do not play any video or audio automatically.

  • My app, website, or content is fully usable with only a keyboard.

  • I do not trap the keyboard except in planned and expected situations (such as a menu or a modal dialog).

  • Any timers or timed elements have controls.

  • Content that moves, such as tickers or slideshows, have controls that let users pause, stop, or even hide it.

  • Nothing in my app or website flashes more than three times per second.

  • I have provided skip links.

  • My website has a clear title on every page.

  • I do not use any positive integers for tabindex and tabbing through my website is intuitive and logical.

  • Every link in my website is clear and can be understood out of context (no, "click here" or "read more").

  • Each web page in my website has a language, and any component that can render a language other than the main language is also defined.

  • Elements don't change when they receive focus or input.

  • Lengthy content is broken up with headings for logical paragraphs or sections.

    • ----

  • Errors are clearly presented and a means of correction is provided.

  • Every form field has a label that references the form field with for or is inclusive and wraps the input.

  • My code is valid and has perhaps passed the W3C Validation tool.

  • All non-decorative elements, text, images, and controls have sufficient contrast.

  • My website or app can be scaled up to 200% without loss of content, context, or function.

  • I do not use any images of text; I use actual text.

  • I offer several different ways to find pages or content.

  • The focus indicator when using a keyboard is visible and clear.

  • My navigation and all of my menus are consistent, along with presentation and controls.

  • Buttons and icons within my app or website are used consistently.

  • I offer suggestions for fixing or resolving errors encountered.

  • I provide elegant error handling to reduce the risk of errors with sensitive data or information.
